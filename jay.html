1. Introduction

The intent of this policy is to establish and support a formal Business Continuity Management ({{organization_abbreviation}}) program framework to enable {{organization_abbreviation}} to continue business operations during foreseen or unforeseen events that impact business processes.

This policy is the foundation of {{organization_abbreviation}}’s Business Continuity Management program. This document establishes minimum requirements and supporting strategies to ensure that the business can provide essential business processes in the event of a disruption to the {{organization_name}}, no matter the cause (Manmade or Nature), including:

  1. Order Processing
  2. Receiving
  3. Distribution and Logistics
  4. Finance
  5. Human Resources
  6. Legal
  7. Facilities
  8. Information Technology

2. Definitions

Refer to the Business Continuity Plan & Disaster Recovery Glossary in the Business Continuity Standard for terms used in this and other Business Continuity Management, Disaster Recovery and Crisis Management policies.

3. Compliance & Reporting

  • Business Continuity Management ({{organization_abbreviation}}) Program Governance
  • A policy has been developed, and documented detailing how {{organization_abbreviation}} will manage, and control identified risks to the Business Continuity (BC) of the Enterprise.
  • The {{organization_abbreviation}} policy will be reviewed and updated at least every 12 months or as operational changes occur.
  • Business Continuity Plans ({{organization_abbreviation}}) must be developed and maintained for each critical functional area of the business as determined by the BIA ({{organization_abbreviation}}).
  • Plans must be reviewed and updated on an annual basis to reflect the current operating environment.
  • The {{organization_abbreviation}} testing program and test results must be reviewed yearly and deficiencies prioritized and addressed. These tests include Business Continuity, Disaster Recovery and Crisis Management.
  • Employees must be trained and made aware of their roles in the implementation of the Business Continuity, Disaster Recovery and Crisis Management Plans.

4. Identification of Key Stakeholders

A list of key internal and external stakeholders are identified, documented and maintained.
The Business Continuity (BC) Stakeholder list, consisting of internal stakeholders, is reviewed and, if necessary updated, minimally every 12 months.
The External key Stakeholder list will be reviewed and, if necessary updated, at least once in every 12 months.

5. Responsibilities

The BC Program Executive (or designee) is responsible for:

  • Identifying and maintaining a list of internal BC stakeholders responsible for each of the following areas of planning:
    • Site Level
    • Business Unit Level
    • Functional Level
    • Vendor Level
    • Ensuring the requirements for a successful BC Program are in place:
    • Senior Management Sponsorship
    • Program funding (Budget)
    • BIA (initial and yearly)
    • Incident (IT Security)/Crisis Management
    • Disaster Recovery Planning (technology)
    • Critical Vendors are identified and integrated into the BC Program planning efforts
    • Maintain the Risk Register for sites serviced by the {{organization_name}}
    • Provide Risk and Impact analysis to the BC Steering Committee related to Natural and Manmade potential or active threats
    • Develop, publish and collaborate on Testing schedules for Business Continuity (BC), Disaster Recovery (DR) and Crisis Management (CM).
    • Provide Business Plan Owners with guidance and assistance in development, review and updates to the BC, DR, and CM Plans (in Fusion BC Software)

6. The BC Executive Sponsor is responsible for:

  • Review of the BC, DR and CM Program efforts
  • Provide a point of escalation for roadblocks to the Program
  • Provide leadership and communicate the needs of the {{organization_name}} to the Program Executive (Change, Additions, Deletions and Direction)

7. The BC/DR/CM Steering Committee is responsible for:

  • Publishing policy and standards/guidance for Business Continuity, Disaster Recovery and Crisis Management.
  • Approving the list of internal BC, DR and CM stakeholders
  • Identifying and maintaining a list of key external stakeholders such as investors, regulators, ratings agencies, distributors, and consumers
  • Provide a point of escalation for planning, training, testing and mitigation efforts
  • Review and Approve the Business Continuity (BC) Program Risk Register as changes occur or at least every 12 Months
  • Review and approval of Program efforts (BC/DR/CM Plans, {{organization_abbreviation}} and Testing) yearly
  • Allocating knowledgeable personnel and enough financial resources to properly implement and manage the program.
  • Reporting status and issues to Risk Committee

8. The BC Plan Owner (or Designee) is responsible for:

  • Validate that all Departmental Staff have reviewed and attest to completion of Training in BC, DR (as necessary) and CM according to assigned responsibilities (identified in the Plans for the department/function)
  • Conducting yearly reviews and updates of the Department plans to ensure they correctly reflect the manner functions are performed. Review and as needed modify the Business Impacts ({{organization_abbreviation}}) on the functions performed, the resources required to maintain or recover the function and that the Recovery Time Objectives ({{organization_abbreviation}}) and Recovery Point Objectives ({{organization_abbreviation}}) are met
  • Identification and review of internal and external dependencies for their functions both up and downstream
  • Review and modify as necessary the departments Crisis Call list
  • Participate and confirm resources are made available for yearly Business Continuity, Disaster Recovery (as needed) and Crisis Management Testing efforts
  • Review and provide approval for their respective BC Plans yearly
  • To Review the Risk Register elements for their office to understand the areas of concern.

9. Compliance

All employees and critical vendors must comply with the policies and procedures developed by the Business Continuity, Disaster Recovery and Crisis Management Program Teams.
Communication and understanding of the requirements associated with the Business Continuity Disaster Recovery and Crisis Management program is validated through testing and recorded for all users. The Business Continuity Program is subject to internal and external audits, the results of which will be provided to the Risk Committee by the Executive Sponsor or Designee.
{{organization_abbreviation}} will, at a minimum, assess and confirm the Business Continuity Plans (BCPs) of its critical vendors (IT and Line of Business) by reviewing the relevant documents, such as the vendor’s Crisis Management Plan ({{organization_abbreviation}} Reports etc.). If these documents are not available, then {{organization_abbreviation}} may request a Business Continuity audit.

10. Policy Exceptions

Any requests for exceptions to this policy must be submitted in writing to the BC Program Executive one month prior to the date of the expected exception. Exception requests will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis and will be authorized only after written approval from the BC Steering Committee.
The list of exceptions will be reviewed annually by the BC Steering Committee and will be used for potential amendments to this policy.

11. Policy Maintenance

The BC Program Executive and BC Steering Committee must review and if necessary, update this policy at least every 12 months.

12. Escalation Process

If you suspect any potential or actual violations of law or corporate policies, it is your obligation to contact {{organization_abbreviation}}’s Business Continuity Executive.

13. Violation of this Policy

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract.

14. Training & Awareness

{{organization_abbreviation}} employees and key stakeholders in the BC Program must be aware of the role they play in development, management and role they are assigned in the event of a crisis affecting or posing a threat to {{organization_abbreviation}}. All assigned stakeholders involved must have received enough training to ensure they understand their specific duties and roles in recovering from any crisis.
